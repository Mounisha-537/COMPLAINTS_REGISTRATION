# Roles & Responsibilities

This document describes the tasks, responsibilities, and operational procedures for all platform actors: Customers (Citizens), Officers (Agents), and Admins.

---

## 1. User (Citizen) Responsibilities

### 👤 1.1 User Registration
* **Action**: Citizens register with their email, phone number, and password.
* **Example Scenario**: *Ravi, a citizen who wants to report a water leakage issue, visits the Online Complaint Portal. He registers as a user by providing his email, phone number, and password to create a secure account. Once registration is successful, he logs in and is greeted with a clean dashboard.*

### 🔍 1.2 Browsing Departments & Complaint Types
* **Action**: Users locate the correct department and division for their complaint category.
* **Example Scenario**: *Ravi browses through various departments—such as Municipal Corporation, Electricity, Police—and filters based on category, location, and urgency level. He selects Municipal Department — Water Leakage.*

### 📝 1.3 Lodging a Complaint
* **Action**: Users fill in the complaint form (Address, City, State, Pin Code, Comments) and attach files.
* **Example Scenario**: *Ravi clicks on "Lodge Complaint," selects the appropriate category, fills in the issue details, and uploads an image showing the leak. Once submitted, he gets a confirmation that the complaint has been received and is under review.*

### ⚙️ 1.4 User Complaint Management
* **Action**: Citizens check statuses, modify drafts, reopen resolved tickets, and communicate directly with assigned officials.
* **Example Scenario**: *Ravi can view the status of all his complaints on the dashboard—Pending, In Progress, or Resolved. He can also edit, cancel, or reopen a complaint if necessary, and communicate with the assigned officer.*

---

## 2. Administrator Responsibilities

### 🔑 2.1 Admin Approval (Background Process)
* **Action**: Admins verify and validate newly registered officer/agent accounts.
* **Example Scenario**: *An admin in the backend reviews and approves officer accounts and departmental access to ensure only verified officials are part of the platform. The admin also monitors misuse or spam complaints.*

### ⚖️ 2.2 Platform Governance
* **Action**: Admins monitor general platform activity, audit actions, resolve disputes, and maintain data privacy compliance.
* **Example Scenario**: *The admin oversees overall platform activity, addresses escalated issues, handles user or officer disputes, enforces policies, and ensures data security and privacy compliance.*

---

## 3. Officer (Agent / Technician) Responsibilities

### 📨 3.1 Complaint Confirmation & Assignment
* **Action**: Officers review incoming citizen complaints and assign field technicians to resolve them.
* **Example Scenario**: *A concerned officer from the municipal department reviews Ravi's submission and assigns it to a field technician. Ravi receives a notification via email/SMS that his complaint has been accepted and assigned with a tracking number.*

### 📊 3.2 Officer's Dashboard & Task Management
* **Action**: Assigned officers track tasks, review cases, log progress, and update ticket states.
* **Example Scenario**: *The assigned officer logs into the dashboard, views the complaint details, and updates the complaint status (e.g., "Technician Assigned," "Work Started," "Resolved"). Officers can also provide notes, attach images, or request additional info.*

### 🛠️ 3.3 Field Visit & Complaint Resolution
* **Action**: Technicians perform physical repairs, log confirmations, and upload evidence of resolution.
* **Example Scenario**: *A technician visits the location, fixes the leak, and updates the status in the system as Resolved. The officer uploads photos of the resolved issue for confirmation.*
